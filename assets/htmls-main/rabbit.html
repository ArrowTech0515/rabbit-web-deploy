        Long-tail keywords are longer and more specific keyword phrases that visitors are more likely to use when theyre closer to a point-of-purchase or when theyre using voice search. Most long-tail keywords have lower search volume than short or head keywords. Theyre a little bit counter-intuitive, at first, but they can be hugely valuable if you know how to use them.
        For example: if youre a company that sells classic furniture, the chances are that your pages are going to appear near the top of an organic search for furniture are not very high, because theres too much competition (this is particularly true if youre a smaller company or a startup). But if you specialize in, say, contemporary art-deco furniture, then keywords like contemporary Art Deco-influenced semi-circle lounge are going to reliably find those consumers looking for exactly that product.
        Obviously, youre going to draw less traffic with a long-tail keyword than you would with a more common one, but the traffic you do draw will be better: more focused, more committed, and more desirous of your services.
        Long-tail keywords are valuable for businesses who want their content to rank in organic Google searches, but theyre potentially even more valuable for advertisers running paid search marketing campaigns.
        Ideas for new keywords: You can get ideas for more keywords using our Related Keywords too, choose one of your keywords in the grid and then choose Related Keywords tab. Another great source of long-tail keywords comes from your competitors, click on the competitors tab and then choose one of your competitors, see what keywords they're using and how they get their traffic.

        Guest blog links in highly ranked blogs and news sites can help your website in a number of ways:

        Increases website traffic

        When you publish a guest guest blog, you can include a link back to your own website in your author bio or within the content of the post. If readers find your post interesting, they may click on the link to visit your website, which can increase traffic to your site.

        Improve search engine ranking

        Guest blog links can also help improve your search engine rankings. If the website that publishes your guest post has a high domain authority and links back to your website, it can signal to search engines that your website is a trusted and valuable source of information, which can improve your rankings.

        Increases brand awareness

        Guest blogging can also help increase your brand awareness by exposing your content to a new audience. If your guest post is shared on social media or other channels, it can reach a wider audience and help more people discover your brand.

        Navigation

        Users can navigate directly to a specific guest blog using its unique link. This is especially useful when sharing content through social media, email, or other channels.

        Builds relationships

        Guest blogging can also help you build relationships with other bloggers and website owners in your industry. By contributing high-quality content to their website, you can demonstrate your expertise and provide value to their audience. This can lead to future collaboration opportunities and partnerships.

        Overall...

        Guest blog links can be a valuable part of your digital marketing strategy, helping you increase traffic to your website, increases brand awareness, improve search engine rankings, build relationships. Backlinks from guest blogs, are considered as signals of authority and trustworthiness by search engines. When reputable websites link to your guest blogs, it enhances your site's credibility.

        Sharing your guest blogs can help in a number of ways:

        Engaging with Your Audience

        Social media platforms provide a platform for interaction and engagement. When you share your guest blog, it gives your audience an opportunity to comment, like, and share their thoughts about the content. This engagement can be valuable as it allows you to connect with your readers, receive feedback, answer questions, and build a community around your blog.

        Building Your Brand

        Facebook for example is one of the largest social media platforms with billions of active users. By sharing your guest blog on Facebook, you can potentially reach a larger audience and increase the visibility of your content. Friends, followers, and others who come across your post can like, comment, and share it, further expanding its reach.

        Social Proof and Credibility

        When your guest blog is shared on Social media platforms and receives engagement in the form of likes, comments, and shares, it can provide social proof and enhance your credibility. People tend to trust content that is shared by others, especially if it comes from their friends or connections. This can help establish your authority in your niche and attract more readers to your blog.

        Targeted Promotion

        Social media platforms allow you to target specific demographics, interests, and behaviors with its advertising features. You can leverage this capability to promote your guest blog to a more targeted audience who are more likely to be interested in your content. This can help attract relevant readers to your blog and potentially increase engagement.

        Driving Traffic to Your Blog

        By sharing your guest blog on Facebook, you can direct traffic to your website or blog. When people see your post and find it interesting, they may click on the link to read the full article. This can generate referral traffic and potentially increase the number of visitors to your blog, which is beneficial for building a readership and increasing your blog's visibility.

        Potential for Viral Reach

        Social media platforms like Facebook have the potential for content to go viral. If your guest blog resonates with the audience and gets shared widely, it can quickly reach a large number of people beyond your immediate network. This can result in significant exposure for your blog and potentially attract a substantial influx of new readers.
